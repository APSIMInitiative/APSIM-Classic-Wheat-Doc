4 Biomass accumulation (Photosynthesis)

The daily biomass accumulation (\(\Delta Q\)) corresponds to dry-matter above-ground biomass, and is calculated as a potential biomass accumulation resulting from radiation interception (\(\Delta Q_{r}\), Equation (4.1)) that is limited by soil water deficiency (\(\Delta Q_{w}\), Equation (4.11)).

4.1 Potential biomass accumulation from radiation use efficiency

The radiation-limited dry-biomass accumulation (\(\Delta Q_{r}\)) is calculated by the intercepted radiation (\(I\)), radiation use efficiency (\(RUE\)), diffuse factor (\(f_{d}\), ), stress factor (\(f_{s}\), Equation (4.5)) and carbon dioxide factor (\(f_{c}\), Equation (4.9)).

\[\begin{equation} \Delta Q_{r}=I\times RUE\times f_{d}\times f_{s}\times f_{c} \tag{4.1} \end{equation}\]

where \(f_{d}\), \(f_{s}\) and \(f_{c}\) are defined in the wheat.xml file. In the current version of APSIM-Wheat, only Leaf produces photosynthate. Diffuse factor (\(f_{d}\)) equals to 1 (), so that Equation (4.1) can be:

\[\begin{equation} \Delta Q_{r}=I\times RUE\times f_{s}\times f_{c} \tag{4.2} \end{equation}\]

4.1.1 Radiation interception

Radiation interception is calculated from the leaf area index (LAI, m\(^{2}\) m\(^{-2}\)) and the extinction coefficient () .

\[\begin{equation} I=I_{0}(1-\exp(-k\times LAI\times f_{h})/f_{h}) \tag{4.3} \end{equation}\]

where \(I_{0}\) is the total radiation at the top of the canopy (MJ) which is directly imported from weather records; \(f_{h}\) is light interception modified to give hedge-row effect with skip row. \(f_{h}\) could be calculated based on the canopy width, but is not used in the current version of APSIM (i.e. \(f_{h}\) = 1). So, Equation (4.3) is reduced to.

\[\begin{equation} I=I_{0}(1-\exp(-k\times LAI)) \tag{4.4} \end{equation}\]

Extinction coefficient (\(k\)) varies with row spacing,

\[\begin{equation} k=h_{e}(W_{r}) \end{equation}\]

where \(W_{r}\) is the row spacing which is specified by the user (in the APSIM interface, the .sim or .apsim file); \(h_{e}\) is a function of rowing spacing which is defined for both green leaf and dead leaves by parameters x_row_spacing, y_extinct_coef in the wheat.xml file (Fig. 4.1) and is linearly interpolated by APSIM. In the current version of APSIM-Wheat, no impact of row spacing is considered (Fig. 4.1)

Figure 4.1: Values of extinction coefficient for different row spacings.

4.1.2 Radiation use efficiency

\(RUE\) (g MJ\(^{\text{-1}}\)) is a function of growth stages which is defined by parameters x_stage_rue and y_rue in wheat.xml (Fig. 4.2) and linearly interpolated by APSIM. In the current version of APSIM-Wheat, \(RUE\) equal to 1.24 from emergence to the end of grain-filling and does not vary as a function of daily incident radiation as in the model NWHEAT.

Figure 4.2: Radiation use efficiency (RUE) for different growth stages.

Actual daily radiation-limited biomass accumulation can be reduced by a stress factor (\(f_{s}\), Equation (4.1) and Equation (4.2)). This stress factor is the minimum value of a temperature factor (\(f_{T,\ photo}\), Equation (4.7)), a nitrogen factor (\(f_{N\ photo}\), Equation (12.4)), a phosphorus factor (\(f_{P\ photo}\)) and an oxygen factor (\(f_{O\ photo}\)).

\[\begin{equation} f_{s}=\min(f_{T,\ photo},\ f_{N,\ photo},\ f_{P,\ photo},\ f_{O,\ photo}) \tag{4.5} \end{equation}\]

No phosphorus stress \(f_{P,\,photo}\) and oxygen stress \(f_{O,\,photo}\) are applied in the current version of APSIM-Wheat. So, Equation (4.5) is reduced to

\[\begin{equation} f_{s}=\min(f_{T,\ photo},\ f_{N,\ photo}) \tag{4.6} \end{equation}\]

\(f_{T,\ photo}\) is a function of the daily mean temperature and is defined by parameters x_ave_temp and y_stress_photo in the wheat.xml (Fig. 4.3). Values are linearly interpolated by APSIM. The temperature stress is applied from sowing to harvest.

\[\begin{equation} f_{T,\ photo}=h_{T,\ photo}(\frac{T_{max}+T_{min}}{2}) \tag{4.7} \end{equation}\]

Figure 4.3: Temperature factor in response to mean daily temperature.

\(f_{N,\,photo}\) is determined by the difference between leaf nitrogen concentration and leaf minimum and critical nitrogen concentration.

\[\begin{equation} f_{N,\,photo}=R_{N,\,photo}\sum_{leaf}\frac{C_{N}-C_{N,\,min}}{C_{N,\,crit}-C_{N,\,min}} \tag{4.8} \end{equation}\]

where \(C_{N}\) is the nitrogen concentration of Leaf parts; \(R_{N,\,expan}\) is multiplier for nitrogen deficit effect on phenology which is specified by N_fact_photo in the wheat.xml and default value is 1.5.

For C3 plants (like wheat), the CO\(_{\text{2}}\) factor of APSIM is calculated by a function of environmental CO\(_{\text{2}}\) concentration (\(C\), ppm) and daily mean temperature (\(T_{mean}\)) as published by

\[\begin{equation} f_{c}=\frac{(C-C_{i})(350+2C_{i})}{(C+2C_{i})(350-C_{i})} \tag{4.9} \end{equation}\]

where \(C_{i}\) is the temperature dependent CO\(_{\text{2}}\) compensation point (ppm) and is derived from the following function.

\[\begin{equation} C_{i}=\frac{163-T_{mean}}{5-0.1T_{mean}} \end{equation}\]

Figure 4.4: CO\(_{2}\) factor in response to the CO\(_{2}\) level (\(C\)) for different mean air temperatures.

The daily diffuse fraction was calculated using the functions suggested by :

\[\begin{equation} \begin{cases} \frac{R_{d}}{R_{s}}=Y_{0} & \qquad for\:\frac{R_{s}}{R_{o}}\leq X_{0}\\ \frac{R_{d}}{R_{s}}=A_{0}+A_{1}\frac{R_{s}}{R_{o}} & \qquad for\:X_{0}<\frac{R_{s}}{R_{o}}\leq X_{1}\\ \frac{R_{d}}{R_{s}}=Y_{1} & \qquad for\:\frac{R_{s}}{R_{o}}>X_{1} \end{cases} \tag{4.10} \end{equation}\]

where

\[\begin{equation} \begin{array}{c} A_{0}=Y_{1}-A_{1}X_{1}\\ A_{1}=\frac{Y_{1}-Y_{0}}{X_{1}-X_{0}} \end{array} \end{equation}\]

where \(R_{o}\) is the daily extra-terrestrial solar irradiance (i.e. top of the atmosphere); \(R_{d}\) and \(R_{s}\) are the daily diffuse and global solar irradiance at the surface, respectively. \(X_{0}\), \(X_{1}\), \(Y_{0}\) and \(Y_{1}\) are four empirical parameters.

\[\begin{equation} \begin{array}{l} X_{0}=0.26,\qquad Y_{0}=0.96,\qquad Y_{1}=0.05,\;and\\ X_{1}=0.80-0.0017|\varphi|+0.000044|\varphi|^{2} \end{array} \end{equation}\]

where \(\varphi\) is latitude.

\(R_{o}\) is derived from this function

\[\begin{equation} R_{0}=\frac{86400\times1360\times(\varpi\times\sin(\varphi)\times\sin(\theta)+\cos(\varphi)\times\cos(\theta)\times\sin(\varpi_{0}))}{1000000\pi} \end{equation}\]

where \(\varpi_{0}\) is the time of sunrise and sunset, which derives from any solar declination (\(\theta\)) and latitude (\(\varphi\)) in terms of local solar time when sunrise and sunset actually occur ()

\[\begin{equation} \varpi_{0}=\arccos(-\tan(\varphi)\tan(\theta)) \end{equation}\]

Solar declination (\(\theta\)) can be calculated by

\[\begin{equation} \theta=23.45\sin(\frac{2\pi}{365.25}(N-82.25)) \end{equation}\]

where \(N\) is day of year.

\(f_{d}\) is calculated by a function of the diffuse fraction which is not implemented in current wheat module, (i.e. \(f_{d}\) = 1).

4.2 Actual daily biomass accumulation

The actual daily biomass accumulation (\(\Delta Q\)) results from water limitation applied on the potential radiation-driven biomass accumulation (\(\Delta Q_{r}\)). This water-limited biomass (\(\Delta Q_{w}\)) is a function of the ratio between the daily water uptake (\(W_{u}\), Equation (11.6)) and demand (\(W_{d}\), Equation (4.12)) capped by

\[\begin{equation} \Delta Q_{w}=\Delta Q_{r}f_{w,\,photo}=\Delta Q_{r}\frac{W_{u}}{W_{d}} \tag{4.11} \end{equation}\]

where \(f_{w,\,photo}\) is the water stress factor affecting photosynthesis (Equation (11.8)); \(W_{u}\) is the actual daily water uptake from the root system (which corresponds to the soil water supply (\(W_{s}\)) capped by \(W_{d}\)), \(W_{d}\) is the soil water demand of Leaf and Head parts ().

When the soil water is non-limiting (\(f_{w,\,photo}\) = 1, i.e. \(W_{d}\geq W_{s}\)), biomass accumulation is limited by the radiation (\(\Delta Q=\Delta Q_{r}\), Equation (4.13)). When the soil water is limiting, biomass accumulation is limited by water supply (\(\Delta Q=\Delta Q_{w}\)).

The water demand (\(W_{d}\), in mm) corresponds to the amount of water the crop would have transpired in the absence of soil water constraint, and is calculated from the potential biomass accumulation from RUE (\(\Delta Q_{r}\), Equation (4.1)). Following , transpiration demand is modeled as a function of the current day’s crop growth rate, estimated by the potential biomass accumulation associated with intercepted radiation (\(\Delta Q_{r}\), see Equation (4.1)), divided by the transpiration efficiency.

\[\begin{equation} W_{d}=\frac{\Delta Q_{r}-R}{TE} \tag{4.12} \end{equation}\]

where \(R\) is respiration rate and equals to zero in the current version of APSIM-Wheat, \(TE\) is transpiration efficiency (Equation (11.2)). See for more details about water demand and supply.

The daily biomass accumulation (\(\Delta Q\)) corresponds to dry matter above ground biomass is limited by the radiation interception (\(\Delta Q_{r}\), Equation (4.1)) or by soil water deficiency (\(\Delta Q_{w}\), Equation (11.9)), so that daily biomass accumulation can be expressed as:

\[\begin{equation} \Delta Q=\begin{cases} \Delta Q_{r}\qquad & W_{u}=W_{d}\\ \Delta Q_{w}\qquad & W_{u}<W_{d} \end{cases} \tag{4.13} \end{equation}\]

where \(W_{s}\) is water supply, \(W_{d}\) is the soil water demand from the shoot, limited by radiation interception ( Section (???)(Crop-water-demand)). In the current APSIM-Wheat, \(W_{d}\) is actually only directly affected by the soil water demand of the leaf ( Section (???)(Crop-water-demand)). \(W_{u}\) and \(W_{d}\) are calculated by soil module of APSIM.
